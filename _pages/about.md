I am Associate Professor in Global Health at the University of Oxford’s Department of Social Policy and Intervention ([DSPI](https://www.spi.ox.ac.uk/)), a Research Associate at the University of Cape Town's Centre for Social Science Research ([CSSR](https://humanities.uct.ac.za/cssr)), and a Fellow at Reuben College, Oxford. 

I study how social interventions improve child and adolescent wellbeing and reduce inequalities in Africa, and how these impacts can be valued in policy decisions. My work considers joint effects across health and education and long-term lifecourse impacts, using methods including social survey analysis, systematic reviews and meta-analysis, simulation modelling, and economic evaluation. I draw on empirical examples such as cash transfers, community health worker programmes, and parenting support.

I work with the [Accelerate Research Hub](https://www.acceleratehub.org/) as well as global impact partners including UNICEF, the Gates Foundation, the World Bank, and government ministries in Ethiopia, Kenya, and Nigeria.

My research has led to over 30 publications, major policy reports, and partnerships that inform programmes reducing inequalities and improving child and adolescent health.
