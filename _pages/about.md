I am Associate Professor in Global Health at the University of Oxford’s Department of Social Policy and Intervention ([DSPI](https://www.spi.ox.ac.uk/)), a Research Associate at the University of Cape Town's Centre for Social Science Research ([CSSR](https://humanities.uct.ac.za/cssr)), and a Fellow at Reuben College, Oxford. 

My research develops new approaches to understanding the long-term social and economic returns of investments in programmes and policies that address the social determinants of child and adolescent health. By integrating population social science, causal inference, simulation modelling, and economic evaluation, I quantify how social interventions generate short-term benefits across health and education, and how these accumulate into long-term improvements in long-term economic wellbeing over the life course. A particular focus of my work is identifying equitable policies that strengthen resilience to climate change. My work helps governments identify and prioritise investments that deliver the greatest long-term value, transforming how public resources are allocated beyond traditional health or budget metrics.

I collaborate with the [Accelerate Research Hub](https://www.acceleratehub.org/) as well as global impact partners including UNICEF, the Gates Foundation, the World Bank, and government ministries in Ethiopia, Kenya, and Nigeria. My work has led to over 30 publications, major policy reports, and partnerships that inform programmes reducing inequalities and improving child and adolescent health.
